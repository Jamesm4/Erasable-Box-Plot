Data Erasure Principal

The data erasure principal is the idea that any ink that is not dedicated to displaying data should be erased. This is an idea strongly advocated for by famed information designer Edward Tufte. He demonstrated this concept by reducing the box plot to its bare essentials. With all of the extra lines removed, it is easier for the viewer to compare the data.
